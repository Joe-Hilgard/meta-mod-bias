Meta Analysis

This simulation study tests:

  1. How publication bias affects ability to detect moderators in meta-analysis

  2. Whether adjustments for publication bias may improve or impair detection of moderators, given publication bias.

Data are simulated according to two scenarios:

Medium effects: Studies have true effects of 0, 0.3, or 0.6

Small effects: Studies have true effects of 0, 0.2, or 0.4

Data are then subjected to publication bias and QRPs

No bias: All study results are reported

Biased: Only 20% of of nonsignificant results are published. 50% of studies involve aggressive p-hacking, 40% use moderate p-hacking.

Number of studies per group is fixed to k = 20

Studies have sample size of minimum 20/cell, maximum 200/cell, average 50/cell.

Analysis

Nave analysis: Meta-analysis tests the moderator effect without adjustment for publication bias

WLS-MRA (single slope): Meta-analysis tests the moderator effect after PET adjustment for publication bias. PET slope is fixed across groups.

WLS-MRA (variable slope): Meta-analysis tests the moderator effect after PET adjustment for publication bias. PET slope may vary across groups. Results are presented in terms of bias, root mean squared error, and Type I/II error.

Results

Medium effects

Group Means: 0, 0.3, 0.6

Publication bias causes overestimation of = 0 and underestimation of mods. This underestimation causes increased Type II error rates for moderators. PET-RMA does not quite undo this bias and can have poor power and efficiency.
